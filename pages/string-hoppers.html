String Hoppers

String Hoppers (also known as "Idiyappam") are a popular Sri Lankan breakfast dish made from rice flour. They are light, fluffy, and served with curry or sambol. These steamed noodle-like discs are perfect for a healthy and satisfying start to your day!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1 cup hot water
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp melted butter or ghee
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (optional, for added flavor)
  • Curry or sambol (for serving)

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine rice flour and salt.
  2. Slowly add hot water while mixing with a spoon until the dough comes together.
  3. Once the dough is smooth, add melted butter and coconut milk (if using) and knead until well combined.
  4. Fill a string hopper press with the dough and press onto greased trays or steaming baskets in a circular pattern.
  5. Steam the string hoppers for 5–7 minutes until fully cooked and tender.
  6. Serve with your favorite curry or sambol and enjoy!

Tips & Variations

  • For a softer texture, add a bit more coconut milk to the dough.
  • Use brown rice flour for a healthier, slightly nuttier flavor.
  • Serve with coconut sambol or any curry of your choice for a perfect meal.

Serving Suggestions

  • Pair with a spicy chicken curry or dhal curry for a traditional Sri Lankan breakfast.
  • Serve with a side of yogurt or chutney for extra flavor.

Nutrition (approx. per serving)

  • Calories: 150–200 kcal (per 2-3 pieces)
  • Protein: 3–4g
  • Carbohydrates: 30–35g
  • Fats: 3–4g
